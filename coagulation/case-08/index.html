Mechanisms of Disease

Why This Case Is a Diagnostic Trap

Assay Interference

Coagulation assays are method-dependent. Phospholipid-dependent clotting tests can be distorted by inhibitors, so the laboratory must know when to confirm results with alternate methodology.

Lupus Anticoagulant

Lupus anticoagulants prolong phospholipid-dependent clotting assays. They usually correlate with thrombosis risk rather than bleeding, and they can interfere with clot-based factor assays.

Low Factor VIII

A very low FVIII result raises concern for hemophilia A, acquired FVIII inhibitor, vWF-related FVIII instability, or assay interference. Method selection changes the differential.

Acquired von Willebrand Syndrome

New-onset bleeding in an older adult with absent vWF antigen, absent activity, absent multimers, and response to IVIG supports acquired rather than inherited disease.
